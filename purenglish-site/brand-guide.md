# PurEnglish Brand Guide

> Reference document for all copy, content, and communication on the PurEnglish site.
> Update this file as the brand evolves.

---

## Who We Are

PurEnglish is the resource library of the English Inspectorate for the Haredi District (משרד החינוך מחוז חרדי). We are the supportive, accessible face of the inspectorate — where counselors provide the materials, tools, and guidance that help English teachers do their best work.

We hold professional standards. We also know that many teachers are just starting their English program, and we are here to walk that road with them.

**Tagline concept:** שותפים לדרך — Partners on the road.

---

## Our Audience

### Primary: Haredi English Teachers
- Teach in Haredi (ultra-Orthodox) schools across Israel
- Range widely in English proficiency — many are beginners or weak speakers
- May be new to running an English program entirely
- Have limited time and mental energy to navigate complex systems
- Expect the site to reflect the values and norms of their community

### Secondary: Other Districts within the English Inspectorate
- Colleagues and supervisors who view the site as a professional reference
- Expect a polished, credible presentation

---

## Community Sensitivities

The site must align with halacha and the norms of the Haredi community:

- **No mixed-gender imagery** — do not use images of boys and girls together outside a clear family context
- **Cultural tone** — avoid language or references that feel overtly secular, "modern," or out of place in a Haredi context. This is a matter of judgment developed over time, not a fixed list of banned words.
- **Inclusive of all levels** — never use language that implies a teacher should already know something, or that makes weaker teachers feel embarrassed

## The Core Goal

When a teacher lands on PurEnglish, we want them to feel:

> "Wow — there's a lot here. I know what there is and how to get it. This can really help me in my teaching. I know better how to teach when I use this site."

Every page, section, and line of copy should serve one of these four outcomes:

---

## Messaging Pillars

| Pillar | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Abundance** | There's a lot here. Teachers should feel the depth and breadth of what's available. |
| **Clarity** | I know what there is and how to get it. Nothing is hidden, confusing, or hard to find. |
| **Relevance** | This can really help me. Resources feel practical and directly applicable to classroom teaching. |
| **Growth** | I know better how to teach. The site makes teachers more capable, lesson by lesson. |

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Attributes (scale of 1–5)

| Attribute | Score | What this means in practice |
|-----------|-------|-----------------------------|
| **Formality** | 3 | Balanced — professional but not stiff. Approachable but not casual. |
| **Authority** | 3–4 | "We know what we're doing, and we're here for you." Not lecturing — guiding. |
| **Warmth** | 4 | Genuinely supportive. Teachers should feel cared for, not processed. |
| **Language complexity** | 2–3 | Simple and clear. Never use a complex word when a simple one works. Stay professional enough for a district-level audience. |
| **Energy** | 3–4 | Practical optimism. Encouraging without being performative or cheesy. |

### We speak as "we"
The site voice is the counselors and inspectorate team speaking together. We are building a community of teachers, not broadcasting at them.

---

## Voice in Practice

### We sound like:
- A knowledgeable colleague who makes things easy to find and easy to use
- A supportive guide who believes in the teacher's ability to grow
- A professional body that takes its standards seriously, without being cold about it

### We do NOT sound like:

| Trap | Example to avoid |
|------|-----------------|
| **Bureaucratic** | "In accordance with inspectorate directives, the following materials are hereby provided..." |
| **Patronizing** | "Even if you're not confident in English, don't worry — we've made it simple enough." |
| **Overly casual** | "Hey teachers! Check out these super fun worksheets 🎉" |
| **Preachy** | "Effective English teaching requires consistent implementation of communicative methodology..." |
| **Salesy** | "These incredible resources will transform your classroom!" |
| **Inaccessible** | Long sentences, advanced vocabulary, unexplained acronyms |
| **Confusing** | Unclear navigation, ambiguous labels, buried content |

## Language Rules

### General
- **Contractions:** Use them. "You'll find" not "You will find." They feel warmer and more natural.
- **Address:** Refer to the audience as "teachers" or "you." Not "educators," not "users."
- **Sentence length:** Keep sentences short. One idea per sentence where possible.
- **Vocabulary:** If a simpler word exists, use it. Avoid idioms and colloquialisms that non-native speakers may not know.

### Calls to Action
- Use plain, direct verbs: **Download**, **View**, **Open**
- Avoid expressions that require cultural familiarity: not "Take it for a spin," not "Dive in"

### Preferred Terminology

| Use | Not |
|-----|-----|
| worksheet | activity sheet |
| lesson | class |
| Download | Get it / Grab it / Take it |
| teachers | educators / users |

### Style
- **Oxford comma:** Yes — "songs, games, and worksheets"
- **Headings:** Sentence case — "Prepared teaching materials" not "Prepared Teaching Materials"
- **Exclamation marks:** Use sparingly. One per section maximum. Never use multiple in a row.
- **Emoji:** Avoid on the site entirely
- **ALL CAPS:** Never use for emphasis — use bold instead
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above

## What Good Copy Looks Like

### Page intro (good)
> "Here you'll find everything you need to teach the All About Me unit — songs, a presentation, flashcards, and a worksheet. All ready to use."

### Page intro (bad)
> "The All About Me unit is a comprehensive introductory module designed to facilitate communicative language acquisition in early elementary settings."

### Call to action (good)
> Download worksheet

### Call to action (bad)
> Grab your copy now!

---

## Navigation & Structure Principles

Teachers have limited time and mental energy. If something is hard to find, they will leave.

- Labels must be instantly clear — no clever names that require interpretation
- Every page should answer: "What is this?" within the first two lines
- Group content logically by how teachers think, not by how we organize internally
- Fewer clicks is always better
